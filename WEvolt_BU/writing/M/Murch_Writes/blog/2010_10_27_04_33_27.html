I toyed with the idea of blogging regularly to accompany the entries, but frankly I'm at such a preliminary stage in how I want to present my work that there is rather little to say.  So this one will stand for awhile as the general summary of what I am doing here on Murch Writes.  For those of you who seem to have begun keeping up with me, thanks.  If what I am offering up now is enough to keep your interest you should be very, very happy people once I properly figure out what I'm doing here.

I'm working on a few projects specifically for WeVolt right now.  With that in mind as I write the pacing will match the update-schedule and the material is varied in mood, characters, and setting.  So far, like the Age of Gods arc I began my WeVolt life with, its the best stuff I have yet written.

For now, more of Jyk, and here is why.  When I decided to jump into WeVolt, as suddenly as I made that leap, it was with a long term view in mind.  Really, the concept for "Murch Writes" goes back quite a way.  I even bought the domain and played around with HTML five or six years back, I just did not have the volume of material to take my shot.

The idea is to give a wider view of my writing than can show up in a novel.  Tolkien had a big effect on my early on, but I was always more drawn to the Silmarillion and all of the behind the scenes worldbuilding stories that were compiled and published.  That is probably because, even then, I was trying to turn daydreams into novels.  

Even if I do not put it in print, I have always run through countless background stories in my head.  I can't just sit down and list out the traits of races, nations, characters, etc - I write it out, developing everything organically.  I create the framework in detail, then let my imagination run with it until something worthy of writing starts to coalesce.

That's where all of this Jyk material really falls.  It's a character study.  An in depth examination of the mindset from which I will be approaching my first novel series.  One version/viewpoint of the mindset, at least (Jyk is one of an ensemble cast of main characters).  The Scullion Warrior, the novel that came out of this character study, also falls in that category, I have decided.  

So, here's the deal: if I am good enough gain a substantial readership on WeVolt over the next few months, I will run the novel here on a five or seven day a week schedule.  This is a year of my labor I am talking about, here.  The first novel I have successfully completed.  A book  for which the creation process was so intensive that the novel nearly buckled under the weight - I had to restructure the whole thing, rewrite half, and perform more revisions than I though possible.  And I will be revising and polishing up until the day it goes live.

So if you have stumbled upon me, click the "read the latest page" pane at the top right and check it out.  The best thing to do is click the << button on the top left of the reader and check out the first story arc.  I wrote it specifically to introduce my world to the internet and it serves that purpose far better than anticipated.  If you like what you find, do me a favor and send a couple of friends my way.  Maybe even throw up a comment, or click the 'like' button (heart) at the top and show me you're out there.

And keep an eye out over the next few weeks, I have some pretty radical ideas brewing.  If I can pull even a couple of them off, this space should get extremely interesting with a quickness.
