24 Hour Comic Book Day!

Two weeks ago, on October 2nd the 24 Hour Comics Day Challenge was met. The challenge? To create 24 comic pages in 24 hours.

 

Comic book rock star, James Riot, creator of The Path spearheaded the challenge. He got the fantastic Pulp Fiction Comics Store in Long Beach (www.pulpfictiononline.com) to host every creator willing to embark on the challenge. He then assembled Pop Galaxy's Matt Kirsch to stream the entire event live connecting creators from across the country for 24 hours into one creative collective consciousness.

James became the banner which all creators rallied behind for the challenge. But it turned out it was more than a challenge. It was a near impossible, mind numbing, mental breakdown inducing marathon of an event.

And it was also an incredible success.

Across the country, artists stepped up to the plate and met the challenge head on. They slammed out their best 24 pages in 24 hours.

James Riot, will be collecting and compiling all of the comics completed at the event into a digital book to be distributed through eBook sales engine WOWIO.com. All the proceeds will go to The Hero Initiative (http://www.heroinitiative.org/), a charity benefiting comic creators in need.

We hope you will join us next year when WEvolt unites creators from across the globe to undertake the 24 Hour Comics Day Challenge!
