Rumble Creator Interview: James Riot

James Riot is a WEvolt living legend. His highly acclaimed comic,THE PATH is an Arthurian/Lovecraftian epic that follows Chance McGavin, a man who may or may not be the reincarnation of King Arthur. THE PATH lives in the WEvolt Top Ten and spends most of its time at number one. James is now reloading THE PATH. James has brought his years of experience to redraw, curate and color this seminal story. It looks and reads better than ever. Now is the time to jump on THE PATH and read one of the best webcomics around.

We sat down with James to find out what makes him tick.

1)    How long have you been creating comics?
As a jackass kid scribbling on paper? Since about 1986. In a semi-professional "I want to do this for the rest of my life" way? Since 1997. I actually had a fairly successful (successful in that it made consistent beer money) webcomic called "Antisocial" from 1998-2002. 


2)    What inspired you to begin graphic story telling?
My earliest memory of wanting to draw comics is from when I was a kid. My grandfather was an artist...a proper artist, not a comic-monkey like me. He was always painting and sketching and I always wanted to do what he was doing because I looked up to him. One day I was given a Captain America comic (the issue where the Falcon gets turned into a werewolf...showing my age), started trying to do that, and have been at it ever since. Comics have this great ability to be simultaneously underestimated and overvalued. I've always found that interesting. 

3)    Quick! Plug your comic like your life depended on it.
Reincarnated King Arthur vs. Lovecraft's Cthulhu Mythos with the fate of reality in the balance. It's got Cthulhu in it. Now, I'm not saying he's going to come after you if you don't read it...but I can't promise that he won't. 

4)    Why did you enter the Rumble? How is it going so far?
Because all the cool kids were doing it. It could be going better, my comic always does better in print than on the web. It doesn't help that my day job is working for an environmental clean-up company, and we've been responding to multiple oil spills in-between a hectic con schedule...so updates have been lacking. 

5)    Could you share some of your strategy for The Rumble?
Bribery. Pure and simple. I've been bribing my readers with free digital downloads each week. 

6)  Any unexpected results from your participation in The Rumble?
I'd love to say yes, but it's gone pretty much how I expected.
